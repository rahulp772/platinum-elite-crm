now we’re getting into the part that actually decides whether your CRM gets used… or ignored by agents.

I’ll be blunt:

If your UX requires thinking, agents won’t use it.
It has to feel like “open → call → update → next” in seconds.

Let’s design this like a high-performing real estate sales floor, not a typical SaaS dashboard.

🧠 Core UX Principles (Non-negotiable)
Speed > Beauty
Minimum clicks (max 2–3 actions per task)
Everything action-driven (not data-heavy)
Mobile-first mindset (agents are on phones)
Follow-ups = primary focus

⚡ Micro UX Details (Game-Changers)

These are small but powerful:

1. Auto-open dialer on click

No copy-paste number

2. Auto follow-up suggestion

After call:
👉 “Set next follow-up?”

3. Missed follow-up warning

Popup:
👉 “You missed 3 follow-ups”

4. Sticky action bar in detail screen

Always visible (call / update)

5. One-click status update

No forms

🚫 What NOT to Do
Don’t make forms heavy
Don’t hide actions in menus
Don’t overload with data
Don’t make agents type too much
